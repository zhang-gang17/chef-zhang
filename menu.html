Seasonal Recommendations

It's winter, which means it's time to buckle up and enjoy yourself some warm hearty meals. The ever-popular tonkotsu ramen will always be a staple, while some homemade chicken noodle soup is sure to soothe your soul as well. If you think you can handle the heat, step-up and challenge the Ma La Xiang Guo.

Entreés

Pasta

Some good old pasta: pick your favorite pasta dish and customize it as you see fit.

Ma La Xiang Guo

Price: Varies

Originating from Chongqing, this stir-fry dish is both spicy and mouth-numbing thanks to its abundance of Sichuan peppercorn and dried chili peppers. Build your bowl choosing from various meat, seafood, and vegetable options. Peppers and Sichuan peppercorn can be adjusted for personal taste and tolerance if desired.

Chow Mein

Price: Varies

Chow mein made with either chicken or shrimp, nappa cabbage, celery, julienned carrots, and beansprouts. Finished with freshly minced garlic and a soy/hoisin sauce mix.

Tonkotsu Ramen

Price: $18

A hearty bowl of tonkotsu ramen with shoyu tare and assorted toppings including seasame seeds, kikurage, nori, aonegi, ajitama, and 2 slices of chashu; additional toppings available upon request.

Shrimp Tofu

Price: $10

A generous serving of shrimp seasoned with cooking wine and delicate tofu. Finished with oyster sauce, minced garlic, and scallions for a warm rice dish with a slight kick.

Chicken Noodle Soup

Price: $8

A soul food classic if you need a pick-me-up, made with brown chicken stock from a whole chicken, carrots, celery, and your choice of pasta noodles.
